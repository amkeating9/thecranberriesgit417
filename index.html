The Cranberries were an Irish rock bank formed in Limerick, Ireland, in 1989. The band features lead singer Dolores O'Riordan, guitarist Noel Hogan, bassist Mike Hogan and drummer.


About

Meet The Band

The band was originally named The Cranberries Saw Us and featured singer Niall Quinn, Quinn was replaced by now lead singer Dolores in 1990. The band classified themselves as a alternative rock group, but incorported aspects of indie rock, jangle pop, dream pop, folk rock, post-punk and pop rock into their sound. In 1991 they released their debut album, Everybody Else Is Doing It, So Why Can't We? Their second album, No Need To Argue (1994), brought the band to internation fame, and included the single "Zombie", which became a stadium anthem and one of the band's most recognizable songs.

Brothers Noel Hogan and Mike Hogan met Fergal Lawler in the mid-1980s, where the young kids grew up in Limerick, Ireland. Later meeting Dolores after putting a advertisement in for a female singer. Mike Hogan later described the meeting as "we were immediately blown away, her voice was something special..she was so small and quiet...then she opened her mouth and this amazing voice, this huge voice came out of the size of her."

Dolores became one of the most recognizable voices in rock in the 1990s, she was known for her lilting mezzo-soprano voice, signature yodel, emphasized use of keening, and strong Limerick accent. Unforuntely she died in January of 2018 during the production of a new album soon to be released. After the passing of Dolores, her bandmates, friends and family, have pulled together a brand new playlist called 'Remembering Dolores' which is available now on all streaming platforms.
